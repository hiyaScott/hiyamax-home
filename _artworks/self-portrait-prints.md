Two self-portrait canvases hung side by side against a warm brick wall. The first piece is a bold black-and-white composition—stark contrasts, decisive strokes, and a quiet confidence in the minimal palette. Above the figure, the name "MAX" floats in muted green, almost like a personal watermark.

The second canvas brings color into the conversation. Soft greens form the face while playful circular stamps—characters and symbols in blue, green, and burgundy—orbit around the portrait like collected memories or favorite things. Where the first piece whispers, this one speaks with a lighter, more playful voice.

Together they capture two sides of the same self: restrained and expressive, serious and playful.
